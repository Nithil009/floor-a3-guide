Section 01

Kitchen Rules

⭐
The #1 Rule
Keep the kitchen spotlessly clean. When you leave, it should look like no one cooked at all. This is the most important rule on the floor.
✅
Wash your dishes immediately after eating. Dry them with the cloth and put them away. No wet dishes in the shelf — moisture damages it.
✅
Clean the sink after use. No food residue, no clogging. Leave it dry and clear.
✅
Wipe spatulas, pots and pans thoroughly before putting them back. Dirty tools affect everyone who cooks after you.
❌
No dishes left in the drying area overnight — and no "quick parking" on the counter. The 5€ rule is in effect: unidentified items left behind = 5€ fee.
❌
Never leave food or items in the kitchen after you're done eating. Take your food with you to your room.
⚠️
If you break a glass — stop, carefully clean all shards, and inform the group chat immediately so others know to be careful.

Waste Sorting

🧑‍🤝‍🧑
Everyone's job
When you throw waste in the kitchen bins, put it in the right bin. The bins in the kitchen are already labelled by type. Sort at the point of disposal — not later.
🗑️
Waste duty person's job
When a kitchen bin is full, take it to the dumping yard and empty it into the correct container. Bring your transponder. Sort carefully — wrong disposal can lead to fines.
📹
There is a camera in the dumping yard. Always sort correctly and dispose waste in the right container.

Which bin for what?

Reference this when you're unsure which kitchen bin to use.

🟤
Bio Waste
Food scraps, peels, coffee grounds, eggshells, organic kitchen waste
🗑️ Bio container
🟡
Plastic & Metal
Plastic packaging, cans, aluminium foil, tins
🗑️ Wertstoff container
⚫
Restmüll
Everything else — mixed non-recyclable waste, paper
🗑️ Restmüll container
🍾
Glass
Glass bottles and jars
🗑️ Glass bins outside (~15 steps)
📦
Cardboard
Flatten boxes before disposing
🗑️ Cardboard bins outside (~15 steps)
📍
Where are the containers?
Bio, Plastic/Metal and Restmüll containers are in the dumping yard (basement, needs transponder). Glass and Cardboard bins are outside, about 15 steps from the dumping yard entrance.

Section 03

Weekly Duties

Duties rotate every week using door signs. The sign tells you what to do, and you pass it on when done.

How the door sign system works:

🚪
Sign appears on your door
🔄
Read the back for instructions
✅
Complete the duty this week
➡️
Pass the sign to the next room
🏖️
Going away for 2+ days? Put the "Absent" sign on your door so the duty skips you that week.

What each duty involves

🗑️
Waste Duty — Empty all dustbins on the floor — including the kitchen bins and the washroom bins — and take bags to the dumping yard. Sort waste into the correct containers. Bring your transponder! Leave bins empty and clean.
📹Note: There is a camera in the yard. Wrong disposal can lead to fines — sort carefully every time.
🛒
Shopping Duty — Check the board checklist. If any shared item (e.g. salt, sponges) is below the required quantity (usually 2–3 packs), buy it. Keep your receipt — the finance handler will reimburse you.
🧺
Kitchen Cloth Washing — Collect the used kitchen cloths from the used tub in the living room and wash them in the washing machine. Once clean and dry, place them back in the fresh tub. There are 3 types: hand towel, dish-drying cloth, and surface cloth — make sure all three are refreshed.

Section 05

Bathrooms & Toilets

We have 2 shared washrooms and 2 shared bathrooms. Keep them clean for everyone.

🚿
After your shower — rinse the shower area using the pipe/hose provided. Wipe away any hair, soap residue or gunk. Leave it as you found it (or better).
🪣
Toilet flush tip: The flush isn't very strong. If needed, use the bucket nearby as a power flush — fill with water and pour it in forcefully to push everything through.
❌
Never leave the toilet clogged without informing the group. Write in the group chat if there's a blockage you can't fix.
🚰
Keep the shower pipe facing INSIDE
After your shower, use the pipe/hose inside the bathroom to rinse the floor clean. Always make sure the pipe nozzle faces inward into the bathroom — never toward the open area inside the bathroom/washroom. If it's left pointing outward and accidentally turned on, water will flood the middle area — and there is no drain there to handle it.
